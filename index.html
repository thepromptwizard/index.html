01

What Is Prompt Engineering?

The Simplest Explanation

A prompt is the text you send to an AI. Prompt engineering is the art and science of writing that text well — so the AI gives you exactly what you need, not just something vague and generic.

Think of it like this: an AI is like an incredibly smart colleague who has read almost everything ever written. But they respond literally to what you ask. If you're unclear, they'll guess. If you're precise, they'll nail it.

Real-World Analogy

You walk into a coffee shop and say: "Coffee."
You might get a random cup. But if you say: "A flat white, medium size, oat milk, no sugar" — you get exactly what you want.

Prompting is the art of being the second customer.

Why Does It Matter?

AI tools like Claude, ChatGPT, and others are becoming part of everyday work and life. The difference between someone who gets mediocre results and someone who gets exceptional results often comes down to one thing: how well they can communicate with the AI.

Prompt engineering skills let you:

What You Gain
  • Save hours by getting good answers on the first try
  • Produce professional-quality writing, code, and analysis
  • Use AI as a genuine creative and thinking partner
  • Build systems and tools that run on AI automatically
No Coding Required
  • No programming knowledge needed
  • Works in any AI chat interface
  • Improves with every prompt you write
  • Transferable across all AI tools
02

Core Principles

Before learning specific techniques, internalize these four foundational principles. Every great prompt follows them.

1

Be Specific

Vague prompts produce vague results. The more specific your request, the more precise and useful the output. State exactly what you want.

2

Give Context

The AI doesn't know your situation unless you tell it. Background information dramatically improves relevance and accuracy.

3

Set the Role

AI models respond very differently when given a role. This is one of the highest-leverage quick wins for beginners.

4

Define Constraints

Tell the AI what you want and don't want. Length, format, tone, what to avoid — constraints are your guardrails.

Role Prompting Example

❌ Without Role

"Explain the risks of starting a business."

Generic response that could apply to anyone.

✅ With Role

"You are a startup mentor who has guided 50+ early-stage founders. Explain the top 5 risks of starting a business to someone with no prior experience."

03

Anatomy of a Perfect Prompt

A great prompt is built from parts, like a recipe. You don't need every ingredient every time — but knowing them all gives you power.

Part What It Is Example
RoleWho the AI should be"You are an experienced copywriter..."
TaskThe actual request (required!)"Write a product description..."
ContextBackground information"...for a handmade leather wallet..."
AudienceWho will read the output"...targeting professionals aged 30–45..."
FormatHow to structure the output"...in 3 short paragraphs..."
ToneThe voice and style"...using a confident, premium tone."
ConstraintWhat to avoid or limit"Do not mention price. Keep under 150 words."

Full Example — All 7 Parts

Assembled Prompt

You are an experienced copywriter who specializes in luxury goods.
Write a product description for a handmade Finnish leather wallet.
The target audience is professionals aged 30–45 who value craftsmanship.
Format it as 3 short paragraphs. Use a confident, premium tone.
Do not mention the price. Keep the total under 150 words.

04

Five Powerful Techniques

These are the most useful prompt techniques for beginners. Each one takes 2 minutes to learn and can immediately improve your results.

1
Zero-Shot Prompting
Simple Tasks

Just ask. No examples, no setup. Done with specificity and context, it works great for straightforward tasks.

Best for: Simple, well-defined tasks with clear expectations.
"Translate the following Finnish sentence to English and keep the formal register: 'Hyvää huomenta, arvoisat vieraat.'"
2
Few-Shot Prompting
Style Matching

Show the AI examples of what you want before asking for the real thing. Incredibly powerful for matching a specific style, format, or pattern.

Best for: Formatting tasks, creative writing with a consistent voice, classification.
// Show examples first, then make your request "I write product taglines in this style: - Dark chocolate: 'Uncompromising. Unapologetic. Unmistakably good.' - Running shoes: 'Built for the road. Designed for life.' Now write a tagline for a Finnish craft gin in the same style."
3
Chain-of-Thought Prompting
Complex Reasoning

Ask the AI to think step by step before giving a final answer. This dramatically improves accuracy for complex problems.

Best for: Reasoning tasks, analysis, problem solving, planning.
// The magic phrase: "step by step" "I'm deciding whether to launch my product on Etsy or build my own website. Think through the pros and cons of each option step by step, considering: no coding experience, small budget, want to test demand first."
4
Role Prompting
Expert Perspective

Assign the AI a specific persona or expertise. It shifts the AI's entire frame of reference and unlocks domain-specific depth.

Best for: Expert advice, specialized writing, learning complex topics.
"You are a senior UX designer with 15 years of experience in e-commerce. Review the following landing page copy and give me three specific improvements to increase conversion rates. Be direct and concrete."
5
Output Formatting
Structure & Clarity

Tell the AI exactly how to structure its response. Unstructured output is harder to use. Formatted output is immediately actionable.

Best for: Any time you need to paste results somewhere or hand output to someone else.
"Analyze these three business ideas and present your analysis as a table with columns: Idea | Market Potential | Startup Cost | Difficulty | Verdict"
05

Do's & Don'ts

Here are the most common beginner mistakes — and how to fix them.

Always Do
  • Start with a role for complex tasks
  • Give background context before your main question
  • Specify the output format
  • Tell it the audience for the output
  • Iterate — refine the prompt if results are weak
  • Ask for multiple options when unsure
  • Include examples when style matters
Never Do
  • Assume the AI knows what you're thinking
  • Write one-sentence prompts for complex tasks
  • Ask multiple unrelated questions at once
  • Accept the first output if it's mediocre
  • Use unexplained jargon or abbreviations
  • Make the prompt so long it becomes contradictory

Before & After: Real Transformations

Example A — Writing Help
❌ Before

"Write me an email."

No type, recipient, subject, goal, or tone. The AI has nothing to work with.

✅ After

"Write a short, friendly email from me to a potential client in Finland, following up after our first meeting last week. Express genuine interest in working together and suggest a call next week. Under 150 words."

Example B — Learning a Topic
❌ Before

"Explain blockchain."

What level? For what purpose? For whom? All unknown.

✅ After

"Explain blockchain to me as if I'm a smart 14-year-old. Use an analogy to something from everyday life. Avoid all technical jargon. Under 200 words."

Example C — Getting Advice
❌ Before

"How do I grow my Etsy shop?"

What shop? What products? What stage? What budget?

✅ After

"I sell digital prompt packs on Etsy. I have 4 listings and 12 sales. My audience is knowledge workers. Give me 5 specific, actionable tactics to get my first 100 sales. Focus on free or low-cost strategies."

06

Practice Exercises

The only way to get good at prompting is to practice. Work through these exercises in order. Each one builds a specific skill.

Exercise 01

The Specificity Drill

Goal: Turn vague prompts into precise, powerful ones.

Take each of these weak prompts and rewrite them using the principles from Section 2:

  1. "Help me with social media."
  2. "Write about Finland."
  3. "Give me advice."

Then test both versions and compare the results side by side.

Exercise 02

Build a Full Prompt

Goal: Use all 7 parts of the prompt anatomy.

Choose a real task you actually need done. Build a prompt that includes all 7 parts: Role / Task / Context / Audience / Format / Tone / Constraint.

Run it. If the output isn't perfect, identify which part was weak and revise it.

Exercise 03

Few-Shot Style Matching

Goal: Train the AI to match your personal style.

Find 3 examples of writing you love — your own or someone else's. Build a few-shot prompt with these examples, then ask the AI to write something new in that same style.

Ideas: A newsletter intro, product description, LinkedIn post, or creative piece.

Exercise 04

Chain-of-Thought Challenge

Goal: Solve a complex problem using step-by-step reasoning.

Pick a real decision you're facing. Ask the AI to:

  1. List all the relevant factors to consider
  2. Analyze the pros and cons step by step
  3. Give a final recommendation with reasoning

Notice how much better the answer is when the AI reasons out loud.

Exercise 05

The Iteration Loop

Goal: Learn how to improve a prompt through iteration.

Write any prompt and run it. Then ask yourself: "What is wrong or missing about this output?"

Change exactly one thing in the prompt to fix it. Repeat 3 times. Track what changed.

This loop — write, evaluate, refine — is how experts get truly excellent results.

Cheat Sheet

Your quick-reference card. Refer to it every time you sit down to write a prompt.

The 7-Part Prompt Formula

1 · Role
"You are a [profession/expert] with [X years] experience in [domain]..."
2 · Task
"Write / Analyze / Create / Summarize / Explain / Review..."
3 · Context
"...for [project / situation / company / product]..."
4 · Audience
"...targeted at [who will read/use this]..."
5 · Format
"Present your response as a [table / list / 3 paragraphs / JSON]..."
6 · Tone
"Use a [formal / friendly / direct / playful / expert] tone..."
7 · Constraint
"Do not [X]. Keep it under [Y words]. Avoid [Z]."
